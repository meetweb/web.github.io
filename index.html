Problem statement

(1) Predicting future crimes defining hot-spot crime areas throughout a major city.

(2) Doing this in an equatable way which is defined by a well-defined equity metric

Regarding this, we have aimed to exploit open crime data from multiple urban areas in order to develop and evaluate machine learning methods with respect to both accuracy and equity. Throughout studying our main problem, some of the key questions to be answered are the following:

• What are the best evaluation metrics for measuring equity in predictive policing? Can simple equity metrics, measuring to what extent patrols are concentrated or dispersed across a city, be improved to account for neighborhood demographics, socio-economics, and crime victimization/underlying need for policing?

• Are there trade-offs between policing accuracy and equity, or is it possible to maximize both criteria simultaneously?

• Do the most effective and most equitable methods remain constant across cities, or if not, can we predict which methods will be preferable given some observable characteristics of a city?
